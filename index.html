*** Part 1: What You Need to Know *** First of all, let me briefly introduce Marking Menu and Radial Menu to you. Marking Menu is a circular context menu that users can select items by moving cursor in different directions. Initially items will be showed up around the cursor as a circle. Then moving cursor to an item means selecting that item, or going to next level under that item with generating a new circle. The new circle for next level will surround the previous selected item, which means it will have a different centre compared with the initial one. Radial Menu also has a circular context interface, however users must always click an item to select or go to next level. When clicking an item for entering next level the content circle will hardly move, which means the centre of Radial Menu should be fixed since initialization.
